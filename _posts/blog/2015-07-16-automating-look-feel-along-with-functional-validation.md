Typically we automate the validation of functionalities - using various options and approaches based on the Test Automation Pyramid. However, there are various reasons why one may want to automate the Look & Feel of their application (web / native)  
  
This post is not about if or when we should automate the Look & Feel aspects of the product. This post assumes you have done the discussions, evaluations and validations required to reach the decision that, yes, we need to automate the Look & Feel of the product under test.   
  
The next question is - how? What are the alternatives of accomplishing this type of automation?  
  
If you need basic validation, there is an open-source alternative - where you can do **_Visual Testing and Validations using PhantomCss_**. [Vishnu](<https://twitter.com/vishnu_narang>) & [Shridhar](<https://twitter.com/_shree33>) spoke about this in [vodQA Pune - Innovations in Testing](<http://essenceoftesting.blogspot.in/2015/05/vodqa-pune-innovations-in-testing.html>). See the [slides](<http://www.slideshare.net/vishnunarang/visual-regression-using>) and [videos](<https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kfgC3EHRNIk>) from their awesome session.  
  
If you need more flexibility and functionality from the Look & Feel automation, and want to do this for cross-browser / multiple devices, then I recommend you look at [Applitools Eyes](<https://applitools.com/>) product. I had have seen a couple of demos from [Moshe](<https://twitter.com/MosheMilman>) and [Adam](<https://twitter.com/carmiadam>) about this product - and they were kind to record one of the demos and allow me to share it with all - you can see the demo [here](<http://youtu.be/jokr0F1BrH0>) on [youtube](<https://www.youtube.com/user/abagmar/videos>).  
  
  


  
[Applitools](<https://applitools.com/>) has a free account for individuals, with of course, certain limitation in usage (not functionality). You can sign-up for your account [here](<https://applitools.com/sign-up/>) and try it out.
