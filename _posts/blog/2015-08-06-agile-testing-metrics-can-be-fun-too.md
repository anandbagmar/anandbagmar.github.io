Metrics are meaningless unless in the right context. In this case, my "right" context is purely a "feel-good-factor".   
  
In April 2011, I published the "[Agile QA Process](<http://www.slideshare.net/abagmar/agile-qa-process>)" paper on [SlideShare](<http://www.slideshare.net/abagmar>). I am very happy to see it has received over 30000 views and has been downloaded over 1400 times!  
  
On a similar note, I created a mindmap for [Test Insanse](<https://testinsane.com/>) \- titled - "[Agile QA - Capabilities & Skills](<http://apps.testinsane.com/mindmaps/software-testing-agile-qa-capabilities-and-skills>)". That also seems to be hitting a good note - with almost 1000 views in under 25 days!  
  
So in this case - **_Metrics are fun_**! I don't mind this ego boost to continue writing more, and sharing more!
