I attended [**vodQA in Chennai**](<http://testing.thoughtworks.com/events/continuous-testing-total-quality-assurance>) on 21st Jan 2012. The event was great. Over 100 passionate testers from Chennai testing community turned up and made sure people presenting were on their toes with excellent questions and great interactions.  
  
  
One of the participants already blogged about it [**here**](<http://savitamunde.wordpress.com/2012/01/22/i-got-hangover-after-attending-vodqa-in-chennai/>). She raises valid observations - and I wish I had the opportunity to speak with her directly to address some of the questions / concerns she raised.   
  
  
In this session, I presented a topic - [**"What is WAAT?"**](<http://www.slideshare.net/abagmar/what-is-waat>) based on my open-source project - [**WAAT**](<https://github.com/anandbagmar>). The slides used in this session are available [**here**](<http://www.slideshare.net/abagmar/what-is-waat>).  
  
  
I am already looking forward to the next vodQA in Chennai. For now, I am preparing for [**vodQA Bangalore - "Agile Testing for Teams and Enterprises"**](<http://testing.thoughtworks.com/events/agile-testing-teams-and-enterprises>). on Feb 11, and then vodQA Pune on Mar 17.
